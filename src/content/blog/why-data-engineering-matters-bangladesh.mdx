Most businesses in Bangladesh collect data — sales records, customer information, inventory logs, financial transactions. But very few can actually use it to make decisions.

The problem is not a shortage of data. It is what happens — or does not happen — between the raw data and the decision-maker.

Raw data from POS systems, ERP exports, or spreadsheets is messy. It has duplicates, inconsistent formats, missing fields, and no structure that a reporting tool or analytics layer can work with. Before any dashboard, model, or insight is possible, someone has to clean, validate, and structure that data.

That process is data engineering. And for most Bangladeshi businesses, it is the missing layer.

At Tritium Global, we have spent years doing exactly this work for enterprise clients — building the pipelines that make data usable. eShoopi is the product we built from that experience, packaged for B2B retailers who need customer intelligence without a six-month IT project.

The opportunity for businesses that invest in data engineering now is significant. Clean, structured data compounds. Every decision made on real data improves the next one.
